SECTION 4: THE CREDIT REPAIR PROCESS

How Does Credit Repair Work? (Explain This Simply)

Under federal law — specifically the Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) — consumers have the right to dispute any information on their credit report that they believe is inaccurate, incomplete, or unverifiable. When a dispute is filed, the credit bureau has 30 days to investigate. If the creditor or furnisher cannot verify the item, it must be removed.

Our 4-Round Dispute System (Accelerated Audit)

  1. Round 1 — Deep-Dive Audit: We pull and analyze all 3 credit bureau reports (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion). We identify every disputable item and create a prioritized dispute plan.
  2. Round 2 — Aggressive Dispute Strategy: We draft customized 609/623 dispute letters for each negative item. Letters are sent via certified mail to the bureaus.
  3. Round 3 — Bureau Processing (30 days): The bureaus investigate. We monitor responses and escalate where necessary.
  4. Round 4 — Results & Score Update: We report deletions and score changes to the client. We re-dispute any items that were verified incorrectly.

What Are 609 and 623 Letters?

  • Section 609 of the FCRA gives consumers the right to request the original documentation supporting any item on their credit report. If the bureau cannot produce it, the item must be removed.
  • Section 623 allows consumers to dispute directly with the original creditor (the "furnisher" of the information), in addition to the bureau. This is a powerful second avenue for dispute.

Why Certified Mail?

Certified mail creates a legal paper trail. If a bureau ignores or mishandles a dispute, the certified mail receipt is proof they received it — which can be used in a lawsuit for FCRA violations (up to $1,000 per violation in statutory damages).

How Long Does It Take?

  • First results typically appear within 30–45 days of the first dispute round.
  • Full 4-round process takes approximately 90–120 days.
  • Complex files (bankruptcy, multiple collections) may take up to 6 months.

SECTION 7: LEGAL KNOWLEDGE BASE

Key Laws You Should Know

FCRA — Fair Credit Reporting Act

  • Governs how credit bureaus collect, maintain, and report credit information.
  • Section 609: Right to request original source documentation for any credit item.
  • Section 611: Bureau must investigate disputes within 30 days (45 days if you send additional documentation).
  • Section 623: Right to dispute directly with the original creditor/furnisher.
  • Penalty for violations: $100–$1,000 per violation in statutory damages; actual damages + attorney fees.
  • Most negative items must be removed after 7 years. Bankruptcies: 10 years.

FDCPA — Fair Debt Collection Practices Act

  • Governs how third-party debt collectors may contact and communicate with consumers.
  • Prohibits harassment, false statements, and unfair practices.
  • Consumers can request a "debt validation letter" — collector must prove the debt is valid.
  • Violations: up to $1,000 per violation + actual damages + attorney fees.
