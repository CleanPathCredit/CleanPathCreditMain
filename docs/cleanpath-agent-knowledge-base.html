SECTION 5: FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: Can you guarantee my credit will improve?

A: We cannot legally guarantee specific point increases — no honest credit repair company can. What we do guarantee is our work: if we don't remove at least one negative item within 120 days on the Accelerated Audit, you get a full refund. Most of our clients see significant improvement.

Q: Can I do this myself for free?

A: The law does give consumers the right to dispute items on their own. The challenge is knowing which items to challenge, how to do it effectively, and how to respond when bureaus push back — that expertise takes years to develop. Our DIY Blueprint ($97) gives you our proprietary templates and system if you want to go that route. Most clients find that professional handling gets better results and saves significant time and frustration.

Q: How exactly do your letters work? What do they say?

A: Our dispute letters use proprietary language and strategies developed over hundreds of cases — that's a core part of what makes our program effective, and it's included when you sign up. I'm not able to share the specifics of our methodology, but your advisor will walk you through the process once you're onboarded.

Q: Which laws do you use?

A: We operate under federal consumer protection law, which gives consumers strong rights when it comes to what can and cannot appear on a credit report. The specifics of how we apply those laws are part of our proprietary process — that's what our specialists are trained in.

Q: Is credit repair legal?

A: Absolutely. Credit repair is a legal right under federal law. What we do is exercise your existing legal rights on your behalf — professionally and effectively. We never do anything fraudulent or deceptive.

Q: Will disputing items hurt my credit score?

A: Disputing does not hurt your score. When negative items are successfully removed, your score typically improves. We advise clients to avoid applying for new credit during the process to keep their file stable.

Q: What if the negative item is accurate?

A: We can only work on items that are inaccurate, unverifiable, or improperly reported. If something is 100% accurate and verifiable, we'll be upfront with you — we won't promise to remove it. Accurate items age off naturally over time, and we can help you build positive credit around them in the meantime.

Q: How do I get started?

A: The best first step is to take our free credit analysis at form.cleanpathcredit.com — it takes about 3 minutes and gives you a personalized recommendation. You can also create a free account at cleanpathcredit.com to access your portal and free guides right away.

Q: What's the difference between Accelerated Audit and Executive Funding Audit?

A: The Accelerated Audit is our done-for-you personal credit repair program. The Executive Funding Audit includes everything in Accelerated, plus monthly strategy calls, a business credit roadmap, and priority advisor access — it's designed for business owners and anyone who also wants to build business credit.

Q: I've been scammed by credit repair companies before. How are you different?

A: That's a fair concern — there are bad actors in this space. Here's what sets us apart: we charge a one-time fee with no recurring charges, we back our work with a 120-day money-back guarantee, we're transparent about what we can and cannot do, and every client has a dedicated advisor — not an automated system. We don't make promises we can't keep.

Q: What credit score do I need to buy a house / car / get approved?

A: Generally, 580+ for an FHA mortgage, 620+ for conventional, 660+ for competitive auto loans, and 700+ for most premium credit cards. Lenders also weigh income and debt levels. Our goal is to get your score above these thresholds with a clean report to match.

Q: My credit is so bad it can't be fixed.

A: I hear that a lot — and I understand why it feels that way. But every credit file has items that can be challenged. Even one or two removals can move your score meaningfully. We've helped clients start in the 400s and reach the 600s. The question isn't whether we can help — it's how much and how fast. Would you like to take our free analysis so we can see what's on your file?
