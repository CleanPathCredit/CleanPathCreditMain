# F&I Dealership Walk-In Scripts (Track A6)

Three-channel outreach playbook for the dealership F&I + credit recovery channel. Same shape as the LO scripts, faster pace — F&I managers see more decisions per day than LOs and have shorter patience for cold pitches. Mirror motion: Bexar County in-person walk-ins, breakfast tacos optional but high-conversion.

**RESPA / state-law caveat.** None of these scripts mention specific dollar amounts paid to the dealer for re-quoted closings. While most auto loans aren't federally-related (so RESPA §8 doesn't directly apply), state kickback / referral statutes still do, and a buyer who cross-sells into a mortgage later creates federal exposure on the original referral chain. Use "Partnership terms discussed during onboarding" as the placeholder language until your attorney has structured the comp model.

## 1. Cold-Call Script (30 seconds — F&I version)

**Use when:** You've identified an F&I manager via Google, dealership website, LinkedIn, or a sales-floor walk-by, and you're calling the dealership main line during business hours (ask for the F&I manager by name, NOT the GM).

```
Hey [First Name], this is Alex with Clean Path Credit. I'll be quick — are you the F&I manager who handles credit-challenged buyers, or is that someone else?

[If yes / if they listen]

Great. One reason for the call: most F&I managers we partner with were watching 25-40% of their bumped buyers walk and never come back. We're a CROA-compliant credit-readiness program in San Antonio that brings those buyers back to your desk re-quote ready in 60-90 days on average. Bilingual, no cost to the dealership, no kickbacks.

I'd love to drop off a one-pager next time I'm in your area, or grab 15 minutes on Calendly to walk you through the handoff. Which works better?
```

**Objection handling:**

- *"We already have a credit-repair partner."* → "Got it. Most F&I managers we work with started with someone — usually a generic dispute mill that took 6+ months and never communicated back to the dealership. If you've got 15 min, I can show you what makes our handoff different. Worst case, you've got a backup."

- *"Send me an email."* → "Will do. What's your best email? And while I've got you — do you have a bumped file sitting on your desk this week? Free pre-screen, 48-hour turnaround, no commitment from you or the buyer."

- *"What does this cost the dealership?"* → "Nothing. The buyer is the client and pays the program fee directly. No advance fees — federal law (CROA) prohibits that anyway. Partnership terms with the dealership we discuss on the onboarding call."

- *"What's in it for me?"* → "Recovered units. Even a 15% recovery rate on bumped buyers is real revenue from deals that today walk out the door. Plus your CSI scores improve — buyers who get a structured next step from your dealership remember you."

- *"Are you taking my customer?"* → "Opposite. The buyer comes back to YOU when they're re-quote ready. The handoff page is co-branded with your dealership. Buyer-dealership relationship stays intact — we're just the credit work in the middle."

## 2. Walk-In Pitch (3 minutes)

**Use when:** You're physically dropping in at a dealership F&I desk. **Best timing:** Tuesday-Thursday, 11am-1pm or 4-6pm (slow showroom traffic windows). Avoid month-end (everyone's pushing units; nobody talks to vendors). Bring tacos or coffee — same play as LO walk-ins.

```
Morning, I'm Alex from Clean Path Credit. Not here to take much of your time — just dropping off this one-pager and breakfast for the team. We help F&I desks save bumped deals. If you've ever bumped a buyer for a 580 score or thin file and watched them walk — that's what we fix.

[Hand over the one-pager and the tacos]

Quick question — when you bump a buyer at the desk for credit, what happens next? Where do they typically go?

[Listen]

Yeah — usually nowhere. They walk, they don't come back, you eat the floor traffic. We're the recovery channel. CROA-compliant, San Antonio based, bilingual. 60-90 day average from bump to re-quote ready. No cost to the dealership, no kickbacks, no compliance issue on your side.

The one-pager's got everything. Calendly link's at the bottom for a 15-minute partnership call when you're ready. And if you've got a bumped file from this morning — send 'em over. Free pre-screen for any buyer you refer.
```

**If they engage and ask follow-up questions:**

- *How do I know it's not a scam?* → "Fair question. Three things: we're registered as a Credit Services Organization with the Texas Secretary of State and bonded under Texas Finance Code Chapter 393 (CSO registration #[NUMBER]). We can't charge advance fees — federal CROA prohibits it. And every buyer has an unconditional 3-day right to cancel. Same legal posture as a registered law firm or any compliant operation."

- *How fast can you fix a 580?* → "Cannot promise a specific score outcome. CROA prohibits guarantees. What we can tell you: we'll do a free 48-hour pre-screen on the file and tell you whether we believe we can help and roughly what the timeline looks like. Most files we run are 60-90 days from intake to re-quote ready. Some shorter, some longer."

- *What if they have ITIN?* → "We work with ITIN borrowers. We also coordinate with mortgage LOs who do ITIN mortgages — same buyer often becomes a future home purchase if you're set up to capture that. Adds revenue paths beyond the auto loan."

- *Can I just take a stack of one-pagers?* → "Absolutely. Take 20. I'll restock you next week. The QR code on the flyer goes straight to the buyer intake — they can scan it from your desk before they walk out."

## 5. F&I Archetype Variations

Same core scripts, lead with archetype-specific pain.

### Archetype A: Independent used-car lots (mom-and-pop)

**Profile:** Family-owned, often single-store. F&I and sales are sometimes the same person (small lots) or 1-2 person F&I team. Heavy bilingual buyer flow in San Antonio, especially Latino working-class first-time buyers.

**Walk-in conversion rate:** Highest of any F&I archetype. Most accessible, fewest gatekeepers, most bumped files per week.

**Lead-in tweak:** *"Most independent lots we work with are bumping at least 30% of their floor traffic for credit. That's gone money. We're the recovery channel — bilingual, no cost to the lot, buyer comes back to YOU when ready."*

**Best book-the-call hook:** "I can do a free pre-screen on any file you bumped this week. 48-hour turnaround. Want to send one over?"

### Archetype B: Franchise dealership F&I (Toyota, Ford, Hyundai, Kia, Chevrolet, etc.)

**Profile:** 1-3 F&I managers per store. Has corporate compliance department — longer sales cycle but bigger volume per signed account. May already have a generic credit-repair partner via a corporate program.

**Lead-in tweak:** *"Most franchise F&I managers we work with already have a corporate credit-repair partner that's slow, English-only, and not built for the bumped Latino working-class buyer profile. We fill that specific gap. Bilingual, San Antonio based, fast pre-screens. You don't have to drop your existing partner."*

**Best book-the-call hook:** "Use us for the borderline Spanish-speaking files your current partner doesn't handle well. Test us for 30 days, no commitment."

### Archetype C: Buy-here-pay-here / subprime auto lots

**Profile:** Already used to working with credit-challenged buyers. Bumps are LESS frequent (their underwriting is permissive) but when they happen, the buyer is deep underwater. Higher CLV per recovered file but smaller volume.

**Lead-in tweak:** *"Most BHPH lots only bump the deepest files. When you do, those buyers are usually 90+ days from re-quote ready, not 30. We specialize in those exact files — deeper credit work, longer programs, but the buyer comes back to you ready for a real loan instead of an in-house contract."*

**Best book-the-call hook:** "You don't lose your current customers — you upgrade them. Buyer who graduates from BHPH to a real auto loan often becomes a referral source for your lot."

**Week 1 totals:** 90 calls, 23 walk-ins, 20 follow-up SMS, 8 Calendly bookings target.

**Conversion expectations (industry typical for B2B cold outreach to dealerships):**
- Cold call to conversation: 10-15% (slightly higher than LO because F&I gatekeepers are looser)

- Calendly to active referral partner: 35-50%
- Net week-1 expected partners: 2-4.

**Combine with the LO motion:** If you're already in the area for an LO walk-in, hit 1-2 nearby dealerships on the same trip. The "Alamo Credit-to-Close" combined motion lets you stack 2-3 walk-ins per location-cluster instead of one.

## 7. Pre-Walk-In Checklist (F&I Specific)

Before the first dealership walk-in, confirm:

- [ ] Texas CSO registration filed and approved (Texas Finance Code Chapter 393)
- [ ] $10,000 surety bond placed and on file with the Texas Secretary of State
- [ ] Attorney-reviewed comp structure (state-level kickback/referral statutes + RESPA-cross-sell exposure)
- [ ] Attorney-reviewed consumer agreement (CROA §405 disclosures, 3-day cancel)
- [ ] Attorney-reviewed fee structure (TSR §310.4(a)(2) — collect after demonstrated results)
- [ ] FCRA permissible-purpose authorization template ready (the buyer signs at intake before any credit pull)
- [ ] Dealership-branded handoff page configured (route like `/r/[dealership-slug]` or generic `/dealership-intake` with optional dealer-name parameter)
- [ ] QR code on the printed flyer points at the right intake URL
- [ ] One-pager printed (50+ copies)
- [ ] Calendly link configured (separate from LO Calendly so bookings tag correctly in GHL)
- [ ] Free pre-screen workflow live (intake form, 48-hour SLA, signed credit-pull authorization)
- [ ] CSO registration number and surety bond carrier name memorized for the "are you registered?" question
- [ ] Spanish-language consumer disclosure available on request (most BHPH lots in San Antonio have heavy Spanish-dominant buyer flow)
- [ ] Smoke-tested funnel end-to-end — dealership handoff path works from F&I desk → buyer intake → first dispute round → weekly status report back to F&I

If any of these is unchecked, **delay the walk-in motion**. F&I managers talk to each other across dealership networks — the first one who asks "are you registered?" and gets a no closes the door across the city.
