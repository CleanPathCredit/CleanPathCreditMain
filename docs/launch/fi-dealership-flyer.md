## Attorney-Review Notes (do not include on printed version)

The following items are deliberately handled this way for legal safety. Do not change them without explicit attorney sign-off.

1. **Comp structure language.** "Partnership terms discussed during onboarding call" is the safe placeholder across both A4 (LO) and A6 (F&I) tracks. While most auto loans aren't RESPA-regulated (so a per-closing fee to the dealer would be RESPA-legal for auto-only), state-level kickback / referral statutes in Texas (Finance Code Chapter 393, OCCC rules on RFCs) still apply, and a buyer who later cross-sells into a mortgage product creates a federal RESPA exposure on the original referral chain. Clean attorney-reviewed comp model first.

2. **No outcome guarantees.** No specific score lift, no specific timeline guarantee, no specific approval guarantee. The 60-90 day language is described as average, not guaranteed. CROA §404(a)(3) prohibits these promises regardless of whether the underlying loan is auto, mortgage, or anything else.

3. **No advance-fee language.** TSR §310.4(a)(2) prohibits credit-repair fees before documented results. Fee structure should be written into the consumer contract as outcome-based.

4. **CSO registration must be live before distribution.** Texas Finance Code Chapter 393 requires registration with the Texas Secretary of State and a $10,000 surety bond before offering credit-repair services in Texas. Distributing this flyer to dealerships before registration is itself a violation — it constitutes "offering" the service.

5. **FCRA permissible-purpose handling.** When a dealership refers a bumped buyer, Clean Path needs the buyer's written authorization before pulling credit. The dealership cannot hand over their already-pulled report (FCRA §616 / §617 violation). The handoff page must include the authorization checkbox at intake.

6. **No auto-broker activity.** Clean Path does NOT advise the buyer on which auto loan to pick, which dealership to use after re-quote, or any auto-financing terms. We restore credit; the dealer / lender handles the loan. Crossing into auto-broker advice triggers Texas DMV / OCCC dealer-licensing exposure.

7. **No TILA advertising trigger.** The flyer does not advertise auto financing terms (APR, monthly payment, down payment). If we ever do, Reg Z §1026.24 advertising rules apply (full TILA-compliant disclosures).

8. **Dealership-branded handoff page — compliance posture.** When we co-brand a buyer-intake page with a dealership, the page must clearly identify Clean Path Credit as the CROA-licensed party providing the credit services. The dealership's branding is for buyer trust, not for misrepresenting who's regulated. Specifically: the CROA Consumer Credit File Rights notice and the 3-day cancellation language must be Clean Path's, in plain English (and Spanish where applicable), regardless of dealership branding.
