# Partner Referral Agreement — Template (RESPA-Safe, No-Comp)

> **⚠️ TEMPLATE — DO NOT EXECUTE WITHOUT ATTORNEY REVIEW**
>
> This is a working draft of a one-way / mutual referral arrangement
> between Clean Path Credit ("CPC") and an outside partner. It is
> structured to be **RESPA Section 8 safe** by design: no fees,
> kickbacks, gifts, or things of value flow in either direction in
> exchange for the referral of a settlement service.
>
> **Pre-execution gates:**
>
> 1. CPC's **Texas CSO registration** under Tex. Fin. Code Ch. 393 must
>    be approved and the surety bond posted before any partner
>    agreement is signed or marketed.
> 2. A **Texas-licensed CFPB/RESPA-experienced attorney** must review
>    and red-line this template before first execution. Partner
>    contracts that look RESPA-compliant on paper but trade per-referral
>    value in practice are how mortgage-adjacent businesses end up
>    paying $10K-per-violation civil penalties + treble damages + criminal
>    exposure.
> 3. The **Schedule A partner-type addendum** (LO/RE/F&I/Other) must be
>    matched to the partner's actual license / NMLS / dealer status
>    before sending — different reps & warranties apply.
> 4. The **Schedule B lead-submission flow** must reference the actual
>    deployed `/partners` intake URL and the consumer-consent capture
>    that lives there.
>
> Comments preceded by `// drafting note:` are **not** part of the
> agreement — they explain why a clause is structured the way it is so
> the reviewing attorney can validate intent.
>
> **This template is general-purpose framework, NOT legal advice. CPC
> is the client; the reviewing attorney is the source of truth.**

### Recitals

**WHEREAS**, CPC operates a credit-services business that helps
consumers identify and dispute inaccurate, incomplete, or
unverifiable items on their consumer credit reports under the Fair
Credit Reporting Act (15 U.S.C. § 1681 et seq.) and the Credit Repair
Organizations Act (15 U.S.C. § 1679 et seq.);

**WHEREAS**, Partner operates a **\[mortgage origination / real
estate brokerage / automotive dealership / other —
specify\]** business and from time to time encounters consumers whose
current credit profile is a barrier to closing a transaction with
Partner;

**WHEREAS**, the Parties wish to make consumers aware of CPC's
services as a non-exclusive resource Partner may mention to such
consumers, and conversely to enable CPC to refer consumers back to
Partner once those consumers' credit profiles support Partner's
services;

**WHEREAS**, the Parties intend that this Agreement be structured to
fully comply with the Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act of 1974
(12 U.S.C. § 2607) (**"RESPA"**), the Credit Repair Organizations Act
(**"CROA"**), the Fair Credit Reporting Act (**"FCRA"**), the
Telephone Consumer Protection Act (**"TCPA"**), and Texas Finance
Code Chapter 393 (**"Texas CSO Act"**), and that no fee, kickback,
gift, or thing of value will be exchanged between the Parties in
connection with the referral of any settlement service or any
consumer;

**NOW, THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants set
forth below and other good and valuable consideration, the receipt
and sufficiency of which are acknowledged, the Parties agree as
follows.

### 1. Mutual Referral Arrangement

**1.1 Partner-to-CPC Awareness.** When Partner, in the ordinary
course of business, encounters a consumer who Partner believes may
benefit from credit-improvement services, Partner may, in Partner's
sole discretion, **inform the consumer of CPC's existence** and
provide the consumer with a Partner-co-branded printable, the
consumer-facing landing page URL, or a business card. Partner shall
not direct, steer, condition, or pressure the consumer to engage
CPC, and shall expressly tell the consumer that the consumer is free
to choose any credit-services provider or to perform credit-dispute
work themselves at no cost under the FCRA.

**1.2 CPC-to-Partner Awareness.** When a CPC client completes their
credit-improvement journey and indicates to CPC that they remain
interested in the **\[mortgage / home-purchase / vehicle-financing /
other\]** transaction Partner offers, CPC may, in CPC's sole
discretion, **inform the client of Partner's existence** as one
option among others. CPC shall not direct, steer, condition, or
pressure the client to engage Partner.

**1.3 No Exclusivity.** This Agreement is non-exclusive. Each Party
may enter into similar arrangements with other parties at any time
and may inform consumers of any number of alternative providers.

**1.4 No Quotas.** Neither Party shall set, track, or enforce a
minimum number of referrals to be made or received under this
Agreement. Failure to make or receive any number of referrals shall
not be a breach.

// drafting note: §§ 1.3–1.4 are RESPA defenses. Exclusivity + quotas
// are the patterns the CFPB cites in §8 enforcement actions as
// evidence that an arrangement is *de facto* a paid referral
// relationship even when no money changes hands. We deliberately
// disclaim both.

### 2. NO COMPENSATION FOR REFERRALS

**2.1 No Fees.** Neither Party shall pay, accept, transfer, or offer
any fee, kickback, commission, bonus, gift, gift card, meal, travel,
tickets, marketing-services payment, lead-purchase fee, sponsorship
fee, or other thing of value to or from the other Party in
consideration of the referral of any consumer or in consideration of
any settlement service rendered (or to be rendered) under or in
connection with this Agreement.

**2.2 No Sham Marketing-Services Payments.** This Agreement is not a
Marketing Services Agreement (**"MSA"**) and does not contemplate any
payment for marketing services. If, at a future date, the Parties
wish to enter into a separate MSA, that MSA shall be a stand-alone
agreement, executed only after the Parties have obtained an
independent fair-market-value benchmark for the marketing services
to be performed, and in no event shall payments under any such MSA
be calculated, varied, or contingent upon the volume or value of
referrals made under this Agreement.

**2.3 Each Party Bears Own Costs.** Each Party shall bear its own
costs of producing, printing, and distributing co-branded materials,
attending joint events, or otherwise marketing the existence of this
arrangement. Neither Party shall reimburse the other for any such
costs.

**2.4 Consumer Pays CPC Directly.** Any consumer who engages CPC's
services as a result of this Agreement shall enter into a separate
written agreement directly with CPC and shall pay CPC's fees
directly to CPC. Partner shall not collect, hold, or remit any
portion of any fee paid by any consumer to CPC.

// drafting note: § 2 is the heart of the RESPA §8 safe-harbor
// posture. The CFPB's PHH Corp consent order (2014) and the §8(c)(2)
// "things of value" framework are the operative law here. The
// attorney should validate that nothing else in the relationship
// (free office space, joint marketing budget, "training" sessions
// that exceed FMV, etc.) is operating as disguised consideration.

### 3. Independent Contractor; No Agency

**3.1 Independent Status.** Each Party is an independent contractor
of the other. Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed to create
a partnership, joint venture, employment relationship, agency, or
fiduciary relationship between the Parties. Neither Party has the
authority to bind the other or to incur obligations on the other's
behalf.

**3.2 No Joint Employer.** Neither Party shall represent to any
consumer, regulator, or third party that the other Party is an
agent, employee, affiliate, subsidiary, parent, or other
related entity of the first Party.

### 4. Compliance with Laws

**4.1 General.** Each Party shall comply, at its own expense, with
all federal, state, and local laws and regulations applicable to its
business, including without limitation those listed in this Section.

**4.2 Partner Compliance.** Partner represents and warrants that:

(a) Partner holds and shall maintain in good standing all licenses,
registrations, certifications, and bonds required to operate
Partner's business in each jurisdiction where Partner does business
(including, where applicable, NMLS registration, state mortgage
broker / loan officer license, real estate broker / salesperson
license, motor-vehicle dealer license, and Office of Consumer Credit
Commissioner registration);

(b) Partner shall not make any oral or written statement to any
consumer that promises, guarantees, or implies a specific credit
score change, item-removal outcome, or qualification result from
CPC's services. Partner acknowledges that such statements would
violate the Credit Repair Organizations Act § 404(a)(3);

(c) Partner shall not make any unfair, deceptive, or abusive act or
practice (UDAAP) statement to any consumer about CPC's services,
fees, timing, or qualifications;

(d) Partner shall not transmit any commercial text message, autodialed
call, or pre-recorded voice message to any consumer in connection
with this Agreement absent the prior express written consent of that
consumer in compliance with the TCPA, the FCC's 47 C.F.R.
§ 64.1200(f)(8), and the CTIA Messaging Principles & Best Practices;

(e) Partner shall not represent, suggest, or imply that engaging
CPC's services is required or conditioned by Partner for the
consumer to obtain Partner's services, financing, or any other
benefit;

(f) Partner shall provide each referred consumer with the disclosure
language attached as **Exhibit 1** (Consumer Disclosure of Voluntary,
No-Compensation Referral) before or at the moment of awareness; and

(g) Partner shall comply with all applicable state credit-services
organization, debt-management, and credit-repair statutes in any
jurisdiction in which Partner refers or markets CPC's services.

**4.3 CPC Compliance.** CPC represents and warrants that:

(a) CPC is and shall remain registered as a Credit Services
Organization under Texas Finance Code Chapter 393 and shall maintain
the surety bond required thereunder;

(b) CPC shall provide each consumer who engages CPC's services with
the Statement of Consumer Credit File Rights, the written contract
required by CROA § 405, the right to cancel notice, and all other
disclosures required by federal and state law;

(c) CPC shall not make any oral or written statement to any consumer
that promises or guarantees a specific credit score change,
item-removal outcome, or qualification result;

(d) CPC shall not collect any fee from a consumer prior to fully
performing the services for which the fee is charged, in compliance
with CROA § 404(b) and the FTC Telemarketing Sales Rule § 310.4(a)(2)
to the extent applicable; and

(e) CPC shall comply with the FCRA, the Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act
Safeguards Rule, and all applicable state privacy and data-security
laws in handling consumer information received from Partner.

// drafting note: § 4.2(b)–(c) shifts the CROA outcome-guarantee risk
// onto the Partner if the Partner makes promises to the consumer that
// CPC would not. Critical for keeping CPC out of joint-and-several
// liability for partner over-promises. § 4.2(e) blocks the worst
// failure mode: a partner conditioning their service on the consumer
// engaging CPC, which would arguably make CPC the partner's required
// settlement service and trigger RESPA §8 even without a direct fee.

### 5. Consumer Consent; Data Sharing; TCPA

**5.1 Consent Before Data Transfer.** Partner shall not transmit any
consumer's personally-identifying information to CPC unless and
until that consumer has provided **express written consent**, on the
record, to the sharing of their information with CPC for the limited
purpose of being contacted about CPC's services. Express written
consent shall be captured by the consumer's affirmative submission
of the CPC-hosted intake form referenced in **Schedule B** or by
another method that meets the requirements of 15 U.S.C. § 1681b
(consumer-report consent) and the TCPA's "prior express written
consent" standard for SMS / autodial.

**5.2 Limited Use.** CPC shall use consumer information received
from Partner solely for the purpose of providing CPC's services and
shall not sell, lease, or otherwise transfer such information to any
third party except as expressly authorized by the consumer in
writing or as required by law.

**5.3 Data Security.** Each Party shall maintain reasonable
administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to
protect consumer information from unauthorized access, use, or
disclosure, in compliance with the GLBA Safeguards Rule (16 C.F.R.
Part 314) and applicable state law.

**5.4 Breach Notification.** Each Party shall notify the other Party
in writing within seventy-two (72) hours of confirming any unauthorized
acquisition of, access to, or disclosure of any consumer information
received under this Agreement.

### 6. CROA — No Outcome Guarantees

**6.1 Mutual Acknowledgment.** Each Party acknowledges that the
Credit Repair Organizations Act (**15 U.S.C. § 1679b**) prohibits any
person from making any statement, directly or by implication, with
respect to a consumer's credit-worthiness, credit-standing, or
credit-capacity that is untrue or misleading or that is intended to
result in the alteration of the consumer's identification.

**6.2 No Specific Outcome Statements.** Neither Party, nor any
employee, contractor, or agent of either Party, shall make any oral
or written statement to any consumer in connection with this
Agreement that:

(a) guarantees or promises a specific number of items to be removed
from the consumer's credit report;

(b) guarantees or promises a specific credit-score increase;

(c) guarantees or promises that the consumer will qualify for any
specific loan product, interest rate, or financing decision; or

(d) guarantees or promises a specific timeline for credit
improvement that exceeds the disclosure that "results vary by
individual circumstance and are not guaranteed."

**6.3 Approved Marketing Materials Only.** Partner shall use only the
co-branded marketing materials approved in writing by CPC (listed in
**Schedule C**). Partner shall not modify, edit, or remove any
disclosure language from approved materials. CPC may, on no less
than thirty (30) days' written notice (or immediately in the event
of a regulatory action or written cease-and-desist), require Partner
to discontinue use of any specific approved material.

Agreement, but excludes information that is or becomes publicly

**8.2 Obligations.** Each Party shall (a) hold the other Party's
Confidential Information in strict confidence, (b) use it only for
purposes of performing this Agreement, and (c) not disclose it to

to know who are bound by confidentiality obligations no less

**8.3 Survival.** This Section 8 shall survive termination of the
Agreement for three (3) years; provided that obligations relating

### 11. Term and Termination

**11.1 Term.** This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and
continues for an initial term of one (1) year (the **"Initial
Term"**), automatically renewing for successive one-year terms
unless either Party gives written notice of non-renewal at least
thirty (30) days before the end of the then-current term.

**11.2 Termination for Convenience.** Either Party may terminate
this Agreement at any time, with or without cause, on thirty (30)
days' prior written notice to the other Party.

**11.3 Termination for Cause.** Either Party may terminate
immediately on written notice if the other Party (a) materially
breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within ten (10) days of
written notice of breach, (b) becomes insolvent or files for
bankruptcy, (c) loses any license, registration, or bond required
for its business, or (d) becomes the subject of a regulatory
investigation or enforcement action relating to the subject matter
of this Agreement.

**11.4 Effect of Termination.** Upon termination, Partner shall
immediately cease using CPC's marks and approved materials and
shall return or destroy all CPC Confidential Information in its
possession. Sections 2 (No Compensation), 5 (Consumer Consent),
8 (Confidentiality), 10 (Indemnification), 12 (Disclaimers), 13
(Dispute Resolution), and 14 (Miscellaneous) survive termination.

upload, or otherwise transmit any consumer's personally-identifying
information directly to CPC. The consumer's submission of CPC's
intake form is the sole mechanism by which the consumer's
information enters CPC's systems.

**B.3 Consent Captured at Submission.** The CPC intake form captures
the consumer's TCPA-compliant prior express written consent to
contact (SMS, email, voice) and the consumer's authorization for
CPC to obtain a soft-pull credit profile on the consumer's behalf.
Partner shall not represent to any consumer that any other consent
mechanism is in effect.

**B.4 Attribution.** If Partner provides Partner with a Partner-specific
URL or QR code, that URL shall include a non-PII attribution
parameter (e.g., `?utm_source=partner-XYZ`) so that CPC can identify
the awareness source for internal reporting purposes. **Such
attribution shall not be used to calculate, vary, or trigger any
payment between the Parties** and is for internal reporting only.

## Drafting Notes for Future Updates (Internal)

1. **MSA escape hatch.** § 2.2 deliberately disclaims any MSA. If at
   a later date there is a real co-marketing budget, draft a
   **stand-alone** MSA — do not amend this Agreement to bolt on a
   payment stream. The cleanest signal that an MSA is real (not a
   sham) is that it can survive losing the referral relationship.

2. **State expansion.** Add per-state riders (Schedule A.5+) before
   marketing in any state outside Texas. Several states have
   mini-RESPA, mini-CSO, or credit-services-specific statutes that
   layer additional reps & warranties.

3. **Spanish-language partners.** Partners marketing to
   Spanish-speaking consumers must distribute Exhibit 1 in Spanish.
   Spanish translation of Exhibit 1 should be reviewed by a
   bilingual compliance reviewer before first use.

4. **NMLS Sales Comparable.** If a regulator ever asks why the
   Partner channel exists without comp, the answer is: "Partner
   regains a customer they otherwise lost; CPC charges the consumer
   directly for actual services rendered. The arrangement creates
   value for both Parties through restoration of the consumer's
   purchasing power, not through the exchange of fees." Document
   this rationale in the file at execution.
